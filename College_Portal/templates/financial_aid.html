Financial Aid & Scholarships

We are deeply committed to making higher education accessible and affordable for all students. Our Financial Aid Office works diligently with students and their families to help navigate the financial aid process and identify various funding opportunities, ensuring a pathway to academic success.

Types of Financial Aid

Grants

Need-based aid that does not need to be repaid. Includes federal, state, and institutional grants, providing essential support without repayment obligations.

Scholarships

Merit-based or need-based awards that do not need to be repaid. Offered by the college, external organizations, and private donors, recognizing student achievements.

Loans

Borrowed money that must be repaid with interest. Includes federal and private loan options, offering flexible repayment plans to suit student needs.

Work-Study

A federal program that allows students to earn money through part-time employment to help pay for educational expenses, offering valuable work experience.

Application Process

To apply for financial aid, you typically need to complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) or your country's equivalent. Our institution may also require additional forms, so please review the steps carefully.

  1. Complete the FAFSA: Submit your Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) online by our priority deadline to maximize your aid eligibility.
  2. Review Your Student Aid Report (SAR): After submission, review your Student Aid Report (SAR) for accuracy and make any necessary corrections promptly.
  3. Respond to Requests: Submit any additional documents or information requested by our Financial Aid Office promptly to avoid delays in processing.
  4. Receive Award Letter: Once your application is processed, we will send you an official award letter detailing your personalized financial aid package.
